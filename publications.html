Machine Learning for Premature Birth Prediction During Pregnancy

Mallipam, Satya Praneeth
ðŸ“š Lecture Notes in Networks and Systems, Springer, 2024

Abstract

This research presents a novel approach to predicting premature births during pregnancy using machine learning techniques. The study employs the MobileNetV2 convolutional neural network architecture for analyzing medical imaging data to identify risk factors and predict premature birth outcomes.

The model demonstrates the effectiveness of transfer learning in medical image analysis and provides a foundation for developing clinical decision support systems for prenatal care.
